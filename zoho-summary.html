Zoho CRM & Zoho Creator – Implementation Overview

This summary explains how Zoho is expected to function in the overall solution. Detailed specifications follow separately.

In one sentence:
Zoho is the orchestration and governance layer that controls lifecycle stages, enforces rules, and provides operational visibility — it is not a document processing engine.

The problem we are solving

We manage onboarding and ongoing compliance for organisations with large numbers of individuals (e.g. learners, beneficiaries, contractors), where progression must be tightly controlled.

The key challenge is not collecting data — it is ensuring that:

  • Steps happen in the correct order
  • Mandatory requirements are not bypassed
  • Exceptions are visible, intentional, and auditable
  • Operations teams can see exactly where things are stuck and why

What Zoho must do well

  • Act as the single system of record
  • Model clear lifecycle stages (from first contact to active)
  • Enforce hard gates between stages based on defined rules
  • Coordinate multiple parallel processes (sales, vetting, finance, ops)
  • Provide dashboards for operational and management oversight

What Zoho must NOT do

  • Perform OCR or document classification
  • Store large volumes of raw documents
  • Rely on manual stage changes as the norm
  • Hide or silently override missing requirements

How Zoho fits with the document platform

An external document platform handles ingestion, OCR, validation, and exception detection. Zoho consumes the results.

  • The document platform reports document status and exceptions
  • Zoho decides whether progression is allowed
  • Zoho controls approvals, overrides, and notifications

Simple rule:
The document platform tells Zoho what is complete or missing.
Zoho decides what is allowed to move forward.

What we expect from a Zoho consultant

  • A clear data model separating companies, individuals, and processes
  • Well-defined lifecycle stages with enforceable rules
  • Thoughtful use of Zoho CRM vs Zoho Creator (not everything in one place)
  • Clean, vendor-agnostic integration design
  • Documentation that allows the system to be supported long-term

How consultants will be assessed

We are evaluating understanding and design quality more than speed.

  • Do you understand where Zoho should enforce rules vs defer to integrations?
  • Can you explain how exceptions flow through the system?
  • Can you design for scale without creating operational debt?
  • Can you communicate clearly with non-technical stakeholders?

If your typical approach relies heavily on manual stage changes or loosely governed workflows, this project may not be a good fit.
